Frequently asked

Questions, answered plainly.

No fine print games. If something here isn't clear, email us and we'll answer honestly.

What exactly am I paying for?

One flat membership: $247 for a full year (about $20 a month). That covers weekly live sessions with Bob, the complete Beacon Academy library, the BEACON 30-Day Navigator's Challenge, private member forums, and the workplace accommodation resource library. No tiers, no upsells, no surprise add-ons.

Do I need a diagnosis to join?

No. Beacon Community is for diagnosed, self-identified, late-diagnosed, and never-diagnosed neurodivergent adults — and for the parents, partners, caretakers, and family who love them. You belong here whether or not you have paperwork.

Is this therapy or treatment?

No. Beacon is a community, not a clinical service. We are not therapists, doctors, or financial advisors, and nothing here replaces qualified professional support. If you're in crisis, please contact a crisis line first — the community can point you to current resources, but it is not an emergency service.

What if it's not the right fit?

You can leave whenever you want — there's no lock-in and no pressure. We'd rather you be here because it's useful than because you felt trapped. Questions about refunds are answered in our refund policy.

Who is Bob, and why should I trust him?

Bob Burr is a Navy veteran and recipient of the Presidential 1,000 Points of Light Award. He's been neurodivergent his entire life, raised a son with Asperger's and dysgraphia who now holds a degree in environmental science and leads wastewater infrastructure for his city, and spent years in transitional ministry walking alongside more than 1,200 neurodivergent individuals. Read his full story →

Will it be overwhelming or high-pressure?

No. The whole point of Beacon is the opposite of that. No countdown timers, no hustle culture, no toxic positivity. The community moves at the speed your brain actually works, and everything is designed as scaffolding you can use on the bad days, not just the good ones.
